Hobbies

When i'm not working, i'm typically filling my time practicing my numerous hobbies. I enjoy staying busy and learning new things, so many of these hobbies respect that.

Programming

I'm currently taking collage level computer science classes at a high school in Colorado. I've had a passion for programming since I first tried it at the age of six. Since then, I've done freelance and personal projects my entire life. Usually I modify the code of games I enjoy, which taught me the basics of several different coding languages. This means I have a diverse portfolio and lots of practice learning new software!

Linguistics

Ever since my first day of learning Swedish, I was hooked. I quickly enrolled in a Swedish school and began studying several hours a day. I later discovered Toki Pona, a minimalist constructed language that makes use of an infinite amount of compound phrases to only use around 150 core vocabulary words. Although it only took me around six months to become fluent, it's helped me with my mental agility and further language learning, two of it's primary goals. Entomology and the evolution of languages continue to fascinate me.

Art and Music

I began learning piano at 7, and absolutely hated it, but as I got older and older I slowly began to appreciate creating music more and more. Now I own over a dozen instruments from all over the world! No matter if it's just a few notes on a kalimba, or trying to compose a masterpiece on a lyre, there's few more soothing things to me than creating music. Art comes from a similar vein. I've taken around four years of art classes, and have improved astronomically. Although traditional art's beautiful, I love making three dimensional art also. My mediums of choice are typically clay, wood, or even just cardboard and duct tape! I hope to gain more ways to express myself artistically in the future.

Sports

Although I wouldn't describe myself as particularly athletic, I've done sports almost my entire life. When I was younger, I'd cycle though various sports, but now i've finally landed on my two favorite. Brazilian jujitsu is a beautiful mix of strategy and agility. Honestly, i'm horrible at it, despite doing it for over a year, but that's the fun part! There's always going to be someone better than you who you can learn from. I've grown so much, even if I have a long way to go. Climbing's a fantastic way to reconnect with nature. Nothing's more exciting than reaching the top of a cliff, and being able to look out into the world. Getting there's no easy task either, but with enough determination and chalk, you'll eventually reach your goals.
